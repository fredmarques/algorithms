# Learning Philosophy

## Why this course exists

This course exists to provide a single, structured path for mastering algorithms and data structures.

It is designed for learners who prefer depth over speed and understanding over memorization.

---

## Fighting brain rot

Modern learning is fragmented.

People jump between videos, blog posts, AI chats and random LeetCode problems.

This course intentionally rejects that approach.

Knowledge should be built gradually.

Each lesson depends on the previous ones.

The curriculum should be followed from beginning to end.

---

## Respecting your time

Every lesson must justify its existence.

- No filler.
- No unnecessary history.
- No repeated explanations.
- One main concept per lesson.
- Estimated duration: 30–60 minutes.

If a topic becomes too large, it should be split into multiple lessons.

---

## Respecting your intelligence

The course assumes the learner is capable of understanding advanced ideas.

Concepts are introduced gradually, but never oversimplified.

The order is always:

Intuition → Visualization → Theory → Implementation → Complexity → Practice.

---

## Active learning

Reading alone is never enough.

Every lesson includes:

- questions
- coding exercises
- implementation tasks
- review
- self-assessment

Learning is demonstrated by building.

---

## External resources

This course is intentionally concise.

When deeper understanding is desired, external resources are encouraged.

Books, papers, documentation and videos complement the curriculum—they do not replace it.

---

## No shortcuts

Skipping lessons is discouraged.

The fastest path is the complete path.

The curriculum has been designed so each lesson prepares you for the next.

---

## Final objective

The goal is not merely to solve LeetCode or pass interviews.

The goal is to become capable of:

- understanding algorithms;
- implementing them from scratch;
- analyzing their complexity;
- recognizing when to use them;
- adapting them to solve new problems.
